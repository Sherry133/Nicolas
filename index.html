Story

I was raised on a farm in the Okanogan Valley of Washington State. One of my responsibilities was to help my father with our expanding vineyard and organic vegetable farm. I loved working outside, watching things grow, helping construct buildings and fixing machinery. My favorite show on TV was "How Things Work" so engineering was a natural career choice for me!

My younger brother and I were sports enthusiasts. We loved snow boarding in the mountains above our vineyard. We also played soccer at the premier level and contemplated tryouts on the University of Washington team. I was Captain of our high school team all four years.

During high school I was President of the Future Business Leaders of America club and took several business courses at the community college in our town. As a member of the Math club I placed 5th in the state in the annual Math contest.

I am grateful to my father and mother, a doctor who delivered more than 1000 babies in the Okanogan, for the support and opportunities they gave me to pursue my dreams and one day excel as a civil engineer.
